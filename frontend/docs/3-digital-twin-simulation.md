# Digital Twin Simulation (Gazebo + Isaac)

Digital twins are virtual representations of physical systems, and simulation environments like Gazebo and NVIDIA Isaac Sim are crucial for developing and testing robots. This chapter explores the concepts of digital twins and their application in robotics using these powerful simulation platforms.

## Introduction to Digital Twins

- Definition and benefits
- Role in robotics development
- Integration with ROS 2

## Gazebo Simulation

- Open-source robotics simulator
- Features: physics engine, sensors, models
- Creating and interacting with simulated environments

## NVIDIA Isaac Sim

- High-fidelity, GPU-accelerated robotics simulator
- Features: realistic rendering, advanced physics, Omniverse integration
- Advantages for AI-driven robot development

This chapter will provide a comparative overview of Gazebo and Isaac Sim, guiding readers through setting up simulations and performing virtual experiments.
