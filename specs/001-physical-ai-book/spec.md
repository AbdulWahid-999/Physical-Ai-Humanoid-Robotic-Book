## User Scenarios & Testing *(mandatory)*

### User Story 1 - Understand Physical AI Foundations (Priority: P1)

A reader wants to understand the core principles, mechanisms, and scientific foundations of Physical AI.

**Why this priority**: Fundamental understanding is the primary goal for the target audience.

**Independent Test**: Reader can clearly explain what Physical AI is and its core concepts.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a reader has completed the relevant chapters, **When** asked "What is Physical AI?", **Then** they can articulate its definition and key components.
2. **Given** a reader has completed the relevant chapters, **When** presented with a new scenario, **Then** they can identify the Physical AI principles at play.

---

### User Story 2 - Grasp Humanoid Robotics Engineering (Priority: P1)

A reader wants to learn about the engineering, design, and system architecture of humanoid robots.

**Why this priority**: Understanding robot construction is a core focus for the target audience.

**Independent Test**: Reader can explain how humanoid robots are built, including design and architectural considerations.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a reader has completed the relevant chapters, **When** asked "How are humanoid robots built?", **Then** they can describe key engineering and architectural aspects.
2. **Given** a reader has completed the relevant chapters, **When** presented with a humanoid robot design, **Then** they can identify its major subsystems and their functions.

---

### User Story 3 - Explore Real-World Applications (Priority: P2)

A reader wants to understand real-world applications and emerging trends in physically embodied intelligence.

**Why this priority**: Practical application knowledge enhances understanding and relevance for the audience.

**Independent Test**: Reader can explain why the field of Physical AI matters and identify its current and emerging applications.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a reader has completed the relevant chapters, **When** asked "Why does Physical AI matter?", **Then** they can discuss its significance and various applications.
2. **Given** a reader has completed the relevant chapters, **When** presented with an industry challenge, **Then** they can suggest how Physical AI might address it.

- **FR-001**: Book MUST cover 10+ major domains of Physical AI with technical depth.
- **FR-002**: Book MUST include 20+ diagrams or structured explanations (text-only).
- **FR-003**: Book MUST cite 12+ academic or industry-grade sources.
- **FR-004**: All claims MUST be supported by evidence or established robotics principles.
- **FR-005**: Book MUST maintain a coherent, structured chapter-based layout.
- **FR-006**: Book MUST be formatted in Markdown, optimized for Docusaurus publishing.
- **FR-007**: Book MUST use APA citation style.
- **FR-008**: All sources MUST be credible, peer-reviewed, or published by robotics research labs.

- What happens if a factual claim lacks traceable sources? (MUST be flagged and corrected)
